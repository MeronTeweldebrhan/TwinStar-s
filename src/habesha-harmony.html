Debate Format

Our structured format ensures productive, balanced, and insightful conversations:

  1. Opening Statements - Each participant presents their initial position (5 minutes)
  2. Moderated Discussion - Guided dialogue on key aspects (30 minutes)
  3. Audience Questions - Community engagement (15 minutes)
  4. Closing Statements - Final thoughts (3 minutes each)
  5. Synthesis - Moderator's summary and action points

Debate Guidelines

  • Focus on Ideas

    Critique perspectives and arguments, not individuals. Foster an environment of intellectual growth and mutual respect.

  • Evidence-Based Discussion

    Support claims with credible sources and research. Anecdotal evidence should complement, not replace, factual information.

  • Cultural Sensitivity

    Acknowledge the diversity within Habesha communities. Avoid generalizations and respect regional differences.

  • Solution-Oriented Approach

    Focus on constructive solutions rather than just highlighting problems. Every challenge is an opportunity for innovation.

  • Language Accessibility

    Debates are conducted in multiple languages with translation support to ensure inclusive participation.
